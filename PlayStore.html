Help Us Launch: Why We Need 14 Brave Testers Before Going Live on Google Play

Building an app is like crafting a spaceship—you can design it, code it, and polish every pixel, but you still need a crew to test the launch sequence. That’s where you come in.

I NEED YOU!

Google Play has a simple rule: before we can officially launch our app to the public, we need 14 real users to install and test it. Not bots. Not simulations. Actual humans who can poke around, explore features, and give honest feedback. It’s part of Google’s effort to ensure apps are stable, useful, and not just vaporware. And frankly, we love it—because it means our launch will be powered by community.

What You’ll Be Testing

This isn’t a bug hunt (though if you find one, we’ll salute you). It’s about trying out core features, spotting any rough edges, and sharing your thoughts—what works, what doesn’t, what could be better. No coding required. Just curiosity and a few minutes of your time.

What You Get

Besides our eternal gratitude? You’ll be part of the founding circle. That means early access to new features, a shoutout in our launch credits (if you want it), and influence over the future of the app.

Why It Matters

We’re not just launching an app—we’re building a community. And that starts with trust, transparency, and collaboration. These 14 testers aren’t just a checkbox for Google—they’re the heartbeat of our development process.

So if you’re reading this and thinking, “Yeah, I’d love to be part of something cool before it hits the mainstream,” then we’d love to have you aboard.

Ready to test? Drop us a message, and we’ll send you the invite link. Let’s build something great—together.
